Defensibility

Six reasons
carriers stay.

Defensibility isn't built on launch day — it's built through data accumulation, operational depth, and switching costs that compound over time.

📈
Data Flywheel
Every route optimized generates training data. More customers → better models → more accurate platform. A competitor starting today cannot replicate years of proprietary fuel route data.
🔒
High Switching Costs
Once routes, inventory, billing, and customer relationships run through POLLY, switching means retraining dispatchers, rebuilding integrations, and losing all operational history.
🔩
Hardware in the Ground
Sensors physically installed at customer tank sites create physical lock-in. Displacing them requires field operations, contractor coordination, and tens of thousands in replacement cost.
🎯
Vertical Specificity
Built around metered tanker trailers, terminal loading dynamics, and fuel delivery windows. Domain knowledge no horizontal competitor can replicate from scratch.
🔗
Multi-Party Chain Lock-In
When POLLY is embedded across carrier, oil company, and customer sites — removing it breaks the visibility chain for all three simultaneously.
🌐
Network Effects
Fuel distributors in the same region share terminals and customer types. Word of mouth in a tight-knit vertical moves faster than any ad campaign.
